the conversation

ownership

Your CV says you cut settlement lag from 26 hours to 40 minutes. What was broken, and how did it become your problem?

From your CV — "cut settlement lag from 26h to 40m, batch → event-driven"

The billing pipeline was a nightly batch job — if it failed at 3am, merchants waited a full day for their money. Nobody owned it. I took it after the third escalation, instrumented it for two weeks, then moved to event-driven settlement in three stages. Lag dropped to ~40 minutes; payout tickets went to zero the next month.

conflict

Tell me about a time you and a colleague genuinely disagreed on a technical call that mattered.

During the migration a senior engineer wanted to keep the batch job as a fallback; I wanted to commit fully to events. We went back and forth. There was tension but we aligned and it worked out.

follow-up

You "went back and forth" then "aligned" — that's the part I want. What did you actually do to get there?

I wrote a one-pager comparing the two failure modes and conceded part of his point — we kept the batch code deployable for one quarter as a lever, just not running.
